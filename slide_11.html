Narration Transcript

Houston Methodist's job architecture has expanded. Every role now includes skills and behaviors alongside the description, experience, and education that have always defined it. That shift puts more in your hands than ever — and the system is built so that you can see it, shape it, and use it.

You don't wait to be developed here. You drive it. There are three places to start.

Card 1 — Keep your Career Profile current in Workday.

Add the skills you bring to your role today. Add experiences and accomplishments that show how you've grown. Keep it current as you take on new work, learn new things, or finish a certification. When your profile reflects where you actually are, your conversations with your leader and mentors land with more weight — and Workday's tools can do more to support your development over time.

Card 2 — Explore your career path in Career Central.

Career Central shows you your current career level, the levels above and below it, and the skill proficiencies expected at each stage. Use the Skills Navigator to see what different roles require. It lets you picture your next step in real, concrete skills you can build — not in the abstract.

Card 3 — Grow with Workday recommendations.

Workday turns the skills on your profile into targeted development. Compare what you have today to the skills required for other roles you're curious about. See where the gaps are. The Career Hub then surfaces personalized recommendations — learning, mentorship, and stretch opportunities aligned to where you want to go. The more you put in, the more the system can do for you.

The biggest shift here isn't the system. It's the mindset. Skills are now part of how every job at Houston Methodist is described — and you are the one who keeps your own picture current. Fifteen minutes in your Career Profile is one of the highest-leverage things you can do for your own growth.

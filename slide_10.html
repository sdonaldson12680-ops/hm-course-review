Main narration (audio_10)

The third and final domain is Execution — and this one is about getting things done, at the highest standard, every time.

Three behaviors live here. Safety and Quality — adhering to every safety policy, identifying hazards before they become incidents, and exercising Stop the Line authority when something isn't right. Problem Solving and Decision Making — understanding a situation fully before acting, staying composed under pressure, and knowing when to escalate. And Business Insight — understanding how your role connects to the bigger picture, staying flexible when priorities shift, and building awareness across departments.

Click each tab to explore what these look like in practice — in clinical settings and non-clinical ones. And notice that Safety and Quality isn't just for clinical staff — every employee owns the safety environment.

Tab 1 — Safety & Quality (audio_10-1)

Safety and Quality means adhering to every safety policy, every time — not just when it's convenient or when someone is watching. It means identifying and immediately reporting potential hazards, near misses, or unsafe conditions before they become incidents. And it means exercising Stop the Line authority when you observe something that puts patient or staff safety at risk. Every employee owns the safety environment. This isn't a clinical responsibility — it's everyone's.

Tab 2 — Problem Solving & Decision Making (audio_10-2)

Problem Solving and Decision Making starts with understanding a situation fully before drawing conclusions. It means staying composed and thinking clearly under pressure — especially in fast-moving, high-stakes moments. And it means knowing when to escalate — when a situation is beyond your scope, asking for help isn't a weakness, it's exactly the right call. Calm, clear, and accountable: that's what this behavior looks like in action.

Tab 3 — Business Insight (audio_10-3)

Business Insight is about understanding the bigger picture — how your role and your daily decisions connect to your department's goals and to HM's overall performance. It means staying flexible and effective when priorities shift. And it means building cross-functional awareness — understanding how other departments work and how they connect to your own. The employees who do this well don't just execute their role — they understand why it matters.

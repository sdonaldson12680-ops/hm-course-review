Main narration (audio_9)

Domain 2 is People — and this one is about how you show up for the people around you every single day.

Three behaviors live here. Communication — not just talking, but really listening. Being present, adjusting to your audience, and making every interaction count. Collaboration and Teamwork — building inclusive relationships, following through on commitments, and bringing kindness to the work even when it's hard. And Developing Self and Others — understanding your own strengths and growth areas, creating a development plan, and saying yes to stretch opportunities before you feel completely ready.

Click each tab to explore specific Leading Self behaviors and see what these look like in practice — across clinical and non-clinical roles.

Tab 1 — Communication (audio_9-1)

Communication is one of the most visible Leading Self behaviors. It means listening attentively and minimizing distractions — putting the phone down and giving your full presence when someone is speaking. It means adjusting your style depending on whether you're talking to a patient, a colleague, or a leader. And it means practicing Heads Up, Phones Down in every interaction, because how you show up physically communicates before you say a word.

Tab 2 — Collaboration & Teamwork (audio_9-2)

Collaboration and Teamwork is about building bridges, not silos. It means seeking out perspectives different from your own and building inclusive relationships across roles and departments. It means following through on commitments reliably — because your teammates are counting on you. And it means bringing kindness and empathy to every team interaction, especially when things are difficult or the pressure is on.

Tab 3 — Developing Self & Others (audio_9-3)

Developing Self and Others starts with honest self-awareness — knowing your strengths and being clear-eyed about your growth areas. It means creating a personal development plan and pursuing it actively, not just during FPR season. And it means saying yes to stretch opportunities — new responsibilities, new challenges — before you feel completely ready. In the next section, you'll see exactly how HM supports this through the FPR process and the 70-20-10 development framework.

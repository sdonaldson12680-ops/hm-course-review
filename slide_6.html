Narration Transcript

Seven service standards. Seven ways you show up for every person who walks through our doors — or works beside you. None of these are new to you. But here's what's easy to forget: they aren't just patient-facing behaviors. They're how we treat each other, too. In a hallway. In a break room. At seven in the morning before the shift really starts.

Heads up, phones down

Be present in the moment. The person in front of you is the priority — whether that's a patient, a family member, or a colleague who needs thirty seconds of your attention.

Smile and greet using the 10/5 rule

Make eye contact at ten feet. Say hello at five. Every person, every time, no exceptions. Two seconds — that's all it takes.

Listen with your ears and eyes

Real listening means slowing down enough to take in what's actually in front of you. Notice it. Then do something about it. Listening with action is care.

You are always on stage

Your behavior is visible — always — and it shapes how people experience this place. When you're at your best in those unscripted moments, that lands too.

Welcome every person and every perspective

Welcome every person fully. Listen to perspectives that aren't your own. Ensure every voice is heard. That's how culture gets built — one interaction at a time.

Personalize the experience

Use someone's name — every time. Remember the detail they mentioned in passing. When someone feels genuinely seen by you, they trust you.

Manage up

Speak positively and proactively about the people around you before anyone has even asked. This isn't flattery. It's trust-building — and it makes every interaction that follows easier.

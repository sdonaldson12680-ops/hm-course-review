Narration Transcript

Let's start with what holds everything else up.

The culture you live at Houston Methodist isn't accidental. It rests on three layers — Mission, Vision, and I CARE Values — that work together in every interaction.

Our Mission — why we exist

To provide high quality, cost-effective health care that delivers the best value to the people we serve in a spiritual environment of caring, in association with internationally recognized teaching and research.

That's the why. It's the reason every role at Houston Methodist exists — clinical or non-clinical, in a patient room or three buildings away.

Our Vision — where we're going

Unparalleled Safety, Quality, Service, and Innovation — simultaneously, always.

Safety comes first. It's the foundation of every decision. And we pursue all four together. Not one at a time. That's what unparalleled means.

Our I CARE Values — how we get there

Five values, in five words.

Integrity. We are honest and ethical in all we say and do.

Compassion. We embrace the whole person and respond to emotional, ethical, and spiritual concerns as well as physical needs.

Accountability. We hold ourselves accountable for our actions.

Respect. We treat every individual as a person of worth, dignity, and value.

Excellence. We strive to be the best at what we do and a model for others to emulate.

You already know these. Take a moment with each one. Click any layer to see how it shows up in your work.

On click — Mission

This is why your role exists — whether you're drawing blood, answering a phone, or restocking a supply cart. Every task you complete is patient care. The mission doesn't live in a boardroom. It lives in the work you do.

On click — Vision

Unparalleled isn't a slogan — it's a daily standard. When you catch a safety issue before it escalates, when you close the loop on a request, when you suggest a better way — that's all four pillars working at once. Not one at a time. Together.

On click — I CARE Values

These five values show up in the smallest moments. Holding a door. Following up on a concern. Owning a mistake. Speaking up when something isn't right. Not five values you wear on your badge — five habits in your hands.

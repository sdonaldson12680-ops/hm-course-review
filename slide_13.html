Narration Transcript

Here's something that surprises a lot of people: most of your growth at Houston Methodist doesn't happen in a classroom or a training program. It happens on the job.

The 70-20-10 framework reflects how people actually develop. Seventy percent of learning comes from challenging on-the-job experiences — stretch assignments, new responsibilities, problems you haven't faced before. Twenty percent comes from the people around you — mentors, leaders, peers who coach, challenge, and reflect with you. And ten percent comes from structured learning — coursework, programs, and on-demand resources like what's available through HMALL.

The insight here is important: most development plans focus almost entirely on the 10%. But the most meaningful growth happens in the 70%. When you sit down for your Focal Point Review conversation, bring a 70-20-10 mindset — most of your development goal should live in what you do, not just what you take.

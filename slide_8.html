Narration Transcript

Let's start with the first domain — Mission, Values and Culture. This is where the I CARE values you just revisited connect directly to the Leadership Behaviors Model.

This domain holds the behaviors that connect Houston Methodist's mission and culture to how you show up every day — regardless of your role. Vision & Values, Change & Innovation, Customer Focus, and Culture & Engagement. Each one has specific Leading Self behaviors and a real workplace example.

Click each tab to see specific Leading Self behaviors and a workplace example. And notice how Customer Focus connects right back to the AIDET and service standards we just covered — it's all one system.

Tab 1 — Vision & Values

Vision and Values is about more than knowing the I CARE acronym. It's about demonstrating those values in every interaction — not just when it's easy or someone is watching. It means learning how your daily behaviors connect to the bigger organizational culture, and reinforcing that culture with your coworkers, even when leadership isn't in the room.

Tab 2 — Change & Innovation

Change and Innovation is about staying flexible when processes shift — and there will always be shifts in healthcare. It means welcoming ideas from others consistently, even when they challenge how things have always been done. And it means using feedback as a signal for growth, not as criticism to resist. The forces shaping healthcare don't slow down — and neither do you.

Tab 3 — Customer Focus

Customer Focus applies to every person you interact with — patients, families, visitors, and colleagues. It means maintaining clear communication about what to expect and when, providing prompt and friendly service regardless of how your shift is going, and following through on every request until it's fully resolved. Sound familiar? It should — this is AIDET and the service standards in action, now with a competency name in the Leadership Behaviors Model.

Tab 4 — Culture & Engagement

Culture and Engagement is about making sure every voice gets heard — not just the loudest ones. It means actively creating space in team discussions for quieter colleagues, seeking feedback from teammates and actually using it, and bringing concerns about exclusion or unfair treatment to a leader in a timely way. This one matters beyond your own work — because culture is built in the small moments, every day.

Narration Transcript

Performance management at Houston Methodist is a cycle, not an event. The Focal Point Review — the FPR — is one moment inside a year-round rhythm. Eight touchpoints in total, organized into three movements.

Walk through them with me. You'll come away with a clearer sense of where you are right now and what's coming next.

Movement 1 — Before the FPR

Two steps lay the foundation. First, requesting feedback — required for nursing staff and strongly encouraged for everyone else. Peer feedback comes back to you in Workday and gives you something concrete to reflect on. Second, your self-evaluation. This is where you reflect honestly on the past year against your I CARE behaviors and the goals you pursued. Be specific. "I demonstrated Accountability by leading the EHR transition for my team" tells a story that "I was accountable" does not.

Movement 2 — FPR season

These four steps are what most people think of when they hear "FPR." They happen in a defined window. Step 3 — required in-service modules. The module you're in right now is part of that. Step 4 — your manager completes their evaluation in Workday. Step 5 — the FPR conversation itself, a two-way dialogue, not a verdict. Come with two or three development goals in mind. Leaders can only support what they know you want. Step 6 — managers communicate merit awards, tied to the work you've demonstrated across the whole cycle.

Movement 3 — All year long

The last two steps continue past FPR season — and this is where most of your development actually happens. Step 7 — partner with your manager on one performance goal and one development item for the year ahead. Remember the 70-20-10 framework. Most growth comes from stretch assignments, not classes. Step 8 — regular check-ins. A 15-minute conversation with your manager every few weeks is worth more than a perfect FPR. Small, frequent touchpoints turn your development plan into actual development.

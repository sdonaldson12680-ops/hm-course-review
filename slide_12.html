Introduction (audio_12-0)

Performance management at Houston Methodist isn't a once-a-year event — it's a continuous rhythm of eight touchpoints that runs all year long. The FPR is where the conversation happens — but everything around it is what makes the conversation meaningful.

Step 1 — Request Feedback (audio_12-1)

The cycle begins with feedback. Peer feedback is required for all nursing staff and strongly encouraged for everyone else — colleagues see things about your work that you can't always see yourself. Request feedback from people who work with you closely and can speak to your I CARE behaviors.

Step 2 — Self-Evaluation (audio_12-2)

You'll receive a notification in your Workday inbox to begin your self-evaluation. Reflect honestly on the past year — your contributions, your I CARE behaviors, the goals you've pursued, and where you've grown. The more specific you are, the more useful the conversation will be.

Step 3 — In-Service Modules (audio_12-3)

Required in-service modules are assigned through the LMS and must be completed to close out the self-evaluation step. This module you're in right now is part of that. Completing it is part of the cycle — not separate from it.

Step 4 — Manager Evaluation (audio_12-4)

Your supervisor completes the manager evaluation in Workday — assessing the same dimensions you reflected on, so both perspectives are ready before the conversation. This is an FPR moment — both inputs come together here.

Step 5 — Performance Conversation (audio_12-5)

The FPR conversation is a two-way dialogue — not a verdict. Together, you and your leader discuss accomplishments, growth areas, feedback received, development opportunities, and possible stretch assignments. Come prepared. This conversation shapes the year ahead.

Step 6 — Merit Awards (audio_12-6)

Managers communicate merit awards to individual team members — tying recognition directly to the performance and growth you've demonstrated over the cycle. This step closes the formal evaluation and connects your work to tangible outcomes.

Step 7 — Goals & Development (audio_12-7)

Partner with your manager on one performance goal and one development item for the year ahead. Use Workday's development tools to keep progress visible and actionable — not just at the next FPR, but throughout the year.

Step 8 — Regular Check-Ins (audio_12-8)

Performance management doesn't end when the FPR closes. Workday enables ongoing check-ins to be captured and documented throughout the year — keeping you connected to goals, feedback, and development without waiting for the next cycle to begin.
